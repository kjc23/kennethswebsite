Mark 11:27-12:17

@March 20, 2025

Theme:

The Pharisees continue to question Jesus’s authority and try to trip Him up but Jesus turns their questions back on them revealing the Pharisees crippling fear of men (and disbelief in God) and Jesus’s authority over all.

Aim:

Those that fear God will inherit the Kingdom of God while those that do not will be thrown out of the vineyard and destroyed. Make the decision to fear God more than man.

Introduction

What makes you feel socially awkward? Why is fear of man so controlling?

Where did we leave off? What happened last week? Where are Jesus and His disciples?

— Read Passage.

— What are the fights with the Pharisees about?

— Any thoughts/questions that jump out.

— Anything about the structure (Mark Sandwich perhaps??)

v27-33

  1. What are Jesus and the Pharisees arguing about in these verses? Authority
  1. What is the purpose of Jesus’s response? Why does He answer the Pharisees question in this way?
  1. What do verses 31 and 32 reveal about the Pharisees? (Who do they fear? Who do they not fear?)

v1-12

  1. Who is this parable directed at?

— Split into groups and compare and contrast Isaiah 5:1-4 with Mark 12:1-9

  1. What is Jesus using this parable for? What is He getting at?
  1. What do the Pharisees really want?
  1. Who are the ‘others’ that Jesus will give the vineyard to?
  1. What is surprising about verse 12? What does it say about the Pharisees?

v13-17

— If Mark Sandwich was not brought up earlier, bring it up here

  1. What is the purpose of the Pharisees questions?
  1. What is so ironic about what the Pharisees say to Jesus in verse 14?
  1. Why don’t the Pharisees believe what they say to Jesus?
  1. How does Jesus demonstrate His authority in this passage?
  1. What does Jesus mean by “Give to God the things that are God’s?”

Application

Why is the fear of man so dangerous?

What might it look like for us to fear Jesus more than we fear man?
