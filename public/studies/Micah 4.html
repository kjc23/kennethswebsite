The Lord’s Established Kingdom (v1-8)

  1. Why do you think it has taken Micah this long (about halfway through the book) to get to God’s promises towards the remnant?
    • Israel’s sin is so pervasive and evil that God must address it proportionately.
    • God knows that those with true faith in Him have not completely died out in Israel though, which is why He now (and briefly in Chapter 2) addresses them, the remnant, telling of how He will rescue them and establish His kingdom.
  1. Since seemingly all of Israel has turned from God and followed their own ways, why does God still have mercy on them? Why doesn’t he wipe them out along with their sin?
    • God stays true to His promises/covenant even when His people do not
    • You would think that once one side of a promise is broken, the whole thing is thrown out, however God continues to stay true to what He promised His people
    • God is a merciful God
      • “You do not stay angry forever but you delight to show mercy” Micah 7v18
  1. How does this remind us of Jesus?
    • Jesus is the fulfillment of God’s promise to His people to save them and reconcile His sinful creation to Himself
    • Even though Jesus was ridiculed, mocked, beaten, and killed, he did not stoop to our level and He upheld His promise to His people
    • Jesus also taught us to “turn the other cheek.” When we are being mistreated, we are not to mistreat in return, rather we should keep ourselves from sin.

    • Ephesians 5v2

    • Matthew 7v24

Israel’s Current Circumstances (v9-13)

v9-10

  1. What is being prophesied in these verses?
    • Israel’s exile to Babylon
    • God saving the Israelites from exile
  1. Israel’s exile is still about 100 years from beginning. Why do you think Micah speaks to them here in the present tense?
    • Speaking in the present tense seems to solidify the certainty with which this exile is coming

v11-13

  1. What is being prophesied in these verses?
    • Nations rising up against Israel
    • Israel defeating the nations and devoting themselves to the Lord

  1. How would the Israelites that heard these prophecies feel?
    • Probably somewhat scared at the prospect of going into exile and nations trying to destroy them but…
    • Hopeful in the Lord’s deliverance (even though they know not when)
  1. What theme is present in the midst of both of these prophesies?
    • The Lord will deliver
    • Evildoers do not know the plans the Lord has (v12)
    • Ultimately God will save His people and the Lord will be glorified forever
  1. How does their experience relate to our situation as Christians today? (Romans 8v18-25)
  1. What impact should our understanding of the future have on our attitudes and actions in the present?
  1. How is this great future going to come about? How do we apply that?
