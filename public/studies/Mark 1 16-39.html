Mark 1:16-39

@October 3, 2024

Theme:

As the Holy one of God, Jesus has authority to teach and call sinners home, as well as having authority over all evil and sin.

Aim:

Follow Jesus trusting his authority and sharing his priority for the Gospel.

General Questions

  1. What was life like for the average Galilean? (5 mins)

    Difficult manual labor every day except for the Sabbath.

    Family-oriented (Andrew and Simon working with their father, Simon’s mother in law)

    Demons, sickness, nobody to help

    What was the Sabbath like?

    Teachers/scribes don’t know what they are talking about (no authoritative teaching)

  1. Read through the passage in groups and highlight any major themes that come up. (5-10 mins)

    Calling disciples, Healings, Unclean spirits, sickness, preaching, Jesus’s Authority

Authority to Call v16-20

  1. What do you notice about the pace of this section? (10 mins)

    Very quick pace (whole passage is quick). “Immediately…”

    1. What does it show about Jesus that Andrew and Simon immediately dropped all that they were doing and followed Jesus?
      Jesus has the authority to call.
    1. What is Jesus asking them to do?

      Leave everything and follow Him

      Jesus has the authority to call at a cost.

Authority to Teach v21-28

  1. How does Jesus’s teaching compare to that of the scribes and pharisees? (3 mins)
    He teaches with authority.
  1. What does it show us about Jesus that even evil spirits listen to His commands? (15 mins)
    Jesus has authority over the spiritual realm.

    He is obviously someone of great power.

    1. Why is the unclean spirit afraid of Jesus?

      The unclean spirit thinks that Jesus has come to destroy it right this moment.

    1. Why doesn’t Jesus “destroy” the unclean spirit?

      This is not the reason that Jesus has come. His priority is to preach.

Authority over Death and Evil v29-34

  1. What more does this section show us about Jesus? (5 mins)
    He has authority over sickness.
  1. What impact has Jesus had on Galilee?

    “The whole city was gathered at the door”

Jesus’s Priorities v35-39

— Narrative slows down —

  1. What does this section tell us of Jesus’s priorities? (7 mins)

    Preaching. Not chasing attention (goes out to desolate place to pray).

    1. After all of this, why would Jesus then leave everybody to go pray?

      Jesus is dependent on the Father.

  1. What has Jesus come to do? (5 mins)

    Preach.

Application

  1. How has this passage added to the way in which you see Jesus?
  1. Based off of this passage, what is Jesus calling us to do now?
