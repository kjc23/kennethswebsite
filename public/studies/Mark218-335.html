Mark 2:18-3:35

@October 17, 2024

Theme:

Jesus is instituting a new Kingdom of God under His authority.

Aim:

Submit to God and follow Christ as a member of his new kingdom family.

— Read Passage —

— Break into groups and search through passage, highlighting who interacts with Jesus, their reactions, and Jesus’s actions —

Jesus vs. the Pharisees (2:18-3:6)

  1. In verses 18-20, who is interacting with Jesus and what are their reactions/Jesus’s reaction?
    1. Does anybody know anything about fasting?

      Mourning and Repentance. Only mandatory on Day of Atonement.

    1. Why then do Jesus’s disciples not fast?

      This is not a time of mourning for them. Jesus is there with them. Why would the friends of the bridegroom mourn in his presence?

    1. What does Jesus mean when He calls Himself the bridegroom?

      He is claiming to be God (Isaiah 64:1-5).

      Keep this in mind as we read the rest of the passage

— Come back to 2:21-22 after going through 3:1-6 —

  1. In verses 23-28, we see Jesus and the Pharisees interacting again. What are the reactions like?

    — Point out, Jesus is not actually breaking the law, just the Pharisaical laws —

    1. Why does Jesus bring up David? (helper question: what do we know about David?)

    — Maybe break into groups for this —

    The Pharisees approve of David, he was a man after God’s own heart yet he broke the law (for a sensible reason), they should therefore approve of Jesus.

    1. What does Jesus mean in verses 27 and 28?

      The Pharisees have gotten the Sabbath (and the law) all wrong. It’s meant to bring them closer to God rather than be simply a rule book.

  1. In verses 3:1-6, who is interacting with Jesus and what are the reactions? What is different about this interaction?

    The Pharisees are ACTIVELY looking for a way to accuse Jesus.

    1. Why didn’t the Pharisees answer Jesus’s question in v4?

      The Pharisees are no longer concerned with doing good. The law is purely surface-level to them.

    — Point out that the Herodians were political enemies to the Pharisees, yet they still work together to kill Jesus —

  1. What does Jesus’s parable in 2:21-22 mean?

    Jesus’s coming kingdom does not fit onto what has been made of His law and people. The old must be thrown out.

    Keep this in mind as we read the rest of the passage.

Sinai - Part Two (v3:7-19)

— Show map, what places do we see? Not just Jewish regions, also Gentiles —

  1. What do we notice about the setting of verses 13-19?
    1. What is this reminiscent of (in the Old Testament)?

      Mount Sinai (Exodus 19:1-6)

    1. Why is that significant?

      Mount Sinai is where God called together the tribes of Israel to Himself for the first time. Jesus is now instating a new Israel made up of those that believe in Him.

Jesus’s Family Mark Sandwich (v20-35)

— Point out Mark Sandwich —

  1. In 3:22-30, who is interacting with Jesus? Where have they come from? How do they feel about Jesus?

    The scribes have come from Jerusalem to see Jesus. Planned attack against Him.

    1. What are the Scribes accusing Jesus of?

      Being possessed by Satan.

— Split into groups to discuss the Strong Man parable —

  1. Who is the strong man? And what is being stolen from him?

    Satan is the strong man and Jesus is binding him up and stealing us back from him.

  1. What does Jesus mean by verses 28-30? (remember to read in context)

    Jesus is saying that those who claim that the work of God is done by and through the devil are so hard hearted that they will never turn and repent.

    v30 - “for they were saying, “He has an unclean spirit.”

  1. What does Jesus mean by v33? What does He not mean?

    He does not mean that His family means nothing to Him. He is introducing the concept of the family of God. Those that believe in Him and do His will are His family.

  1. Why does Mark sandwich Jesus’s discourse with the Pharisees with passages about who Jesus’s family is?

    Emphasizes that Jesus’s family is made up of those that have been saved by Him from the devil. We are part of Jesus’s family.

  1. How does this tie into the themes that we have seen throughout the passage?

    Jesus is creating a new Kingdom that is populated with those that believe in Him and do His will rather than those that are physically born into Israel.

Application

  1. Why does all of this matter?
    1. Why would it be surprising to the Pharisees that the new Kingdom is made up of those that simply believe?
    1. Why is this important for us today?
