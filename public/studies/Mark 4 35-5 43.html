Mark 4:35-5:43

@November 7, 2024

Theme:

Jesus has power over creation, evil, sickness, and even death, even still, only some will respond in faith.

Aim:

See the truth of Jesus’s words and the power that He has over the seemingly hopeless situations in our lives and respond with faith in the Lord our God.

Opening Question

In what situations do we find it hardest to believe that things will work out? (I would say “find it hardest to trust Jesus” but we have one or two non-christians in our group)

— Ask someone to recap what we saw last week —

— Read Passage —

—

Break into Groups and highlight in each section:

  • What is the big challenge to trusting Jesus?
  • What is their response to Jesus?

—

  1. What kind of situations do we see that caused people to doubt Jesus?

    Storms, demons, sickness, death

    1. What kind of responses do we see to Jesus?

      Fear or faith

Jesus Calms the Storm (v4:35-41)

  1. Fill in section of table:
    1. What evidence of hopelessness is there?

      There is a storm big enough that even professional sailors are frightened.

    1. Why would faith be difficult for the disciples?

      Jesus was asleep. He seemed to not care.

    1. What evidence of a miracle is there?

      Jesus says a word and the storms calms.

      1. What did the disciples expect Jesus to do when they woke Him up?
      1. Did they think that He was going to calm the storm?

        It does not seem like it as they were surprised that Jesus did so. They thought that Jesus didn’t care that they were close to death.

      1. Does anybody know of anywhere else in the Bible that we see this imagery of dark stormy waters?

        Genesis in the account of Creation.

      1. What does this tell us about Jesus?

        Jesus is the Creator. He is God.

    1. What reactions do we see from the disciples?

      They fear Jesus but ask “Who is this?”

      1. What’s significant about the disciples reaction?

        They are the people who have been with Jesus the longest but they still fear.

Jesus vs. Legion (5:1-20)

  1. Fill in section of table:
    1. What evidence is there of hopelessness?

      The demon possessed man lives in the cemetery (around death) and is unchainable and seemingly unfixable.

    1. Why is faith difficult in this situation?
      1. What is the setting of this section? And why is it significant?

        The Tombs. Not only are they around physical death, the man being demon-possessed shows spiritual death.

    1. What is the evidence of a miracle?

      Man sitting dressed and in his right mind again.

      1. What does this miracle show about Jesus?

        He has power over spirits/spiritual death.

      1. Why does Jesus let the demons go into the pigs and drown them?

        It is not entirely clear Jesus’s reasoning, but we can say that Jesus sees that dealing with spiritual evil is more important than the farmer’s livestock.

    1. What responses do we see?

      Fear AND Faith. Most want Jesus to leave, but the once demon possessed man wants to follow Jesus.

      1. How does this connect to what we saw last week?

        Last time, Jesus was telling us about the different ways that people would respond to Him. Now we are seeing that in action.

Jairus’s Daughter and the Bleeding Woman (5:23-43)

— Point out Mark Sandwich —

  1. Fill in section of table about Bleeding Woman (24b-34):
    1. What evidence of hopelessness is there?

      The woman has been suffering for 12 years with no end in sight. She would be a social outcast. Could not go to the temple.

    1. Why would it be difficult to have faith?

      Jesus was unaware about the woman and her condition.

    1. What evidence is there of a miracle?

      The woman immediately stopped bleeding. Jesus senses power leaving from Him.

      1. Why does Jesus call the woman out?

        To make her seen. She has been shunned and outcast for many years. Could also be condemnation upon the crowd for not helping her to Him.

      1. How do we see Jesus respond/interact with the woman?

        He calls her daughter and says “go in peace.” It is a very warm and caring interaction.

      1. What does this tell us about Jesus?

        He cares. He is not just going around healing people for His own gratification, He has compassion on these people and cares warmly for them.

      1. What does this miracle in general show us about Jesus?

        He has power over sickness.

    1. What response do we see in this section?

      Both fear and faith. The woman has faith that Jesus can heal her, but is afraid of Jesus.

  1. Fill in section of table about Jairus's daughter (21-23 & 35-43):
    1. What evidence of hopelessness is there?

      The daughter has died. It no longer seems worth it to bother Jesus.

    1. Why would faith be hard in this situation?

      Jesus was delayed.

    1. What evidence is there of a miracle?

      Jesus raised the girl from the dead.

      1. What does this miracle tell us about Jesus?

        Jesus has power even over death.

    1. What responses do we see?

      First, the people laugh at Jesus when He suggests that the girl is just sleeping. Then they are amazed when the girl is brought back to life.

      1. Why does Jesus charge them that nobody should know about what happened? Why was the demon possessed man allowed to spread the word, but not them?

        Gentile Region: DO tell everybody

        Jewish Region: DO NOT tell anybody

        In the Jewish Regions, it was likely that people would misconstrue what Jesus was doing and His purposes. Not to mention, the Jewish councils wanted Jesus dead.
