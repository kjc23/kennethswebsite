Mark 6:53-7:23

@December 5, 2024

Theme:

Jesus condemns those that misuse the law for their own gain (the Pharisees) and shows the heart behind the law regarding defilement.

Aim:

Jesus shows us that sin is really what defiles us and we should humbly avoid putting ourselves in God’s place like the Pharisees did.

Introduction Question

What would most people say are the biggest problems the world faces today? (In Groups?)

War, climate change, homelessness, etc.

— Read Passage —

— Break into groups and highlight repeated words and anything that pops out at you. —

What does defiled mean?

damage the purity or appearance of; mar or spoil.

Does anyone know where else we see it in the Bible?

Seen in the Levitical law.

v1-5

  1. Thus far, what has been our opinion of the Pharisees?

    Not great, they want to kill Jesus.

  1. What word is repeated most in this section?

    Tradition.

    1. Why is this important?

      The Pharisees’s tradition is NOT the law.

  1. How do the Pharisees think one becomes defiled?

    Through physical, external means.

    With this in mind…

  1. What does the Pharisees’s view of defilement show us about their hearts and priorities? (Groups)

    Think that defilement (sin) can be washed off easily by oneself. Their priority is their own traditions, not the law.

v6-13

  1. How does Jesus respond to the Pharisees’s accusation?

    Very severely. He calls them hypocrites and recites a passage from Isaiah.

— Read Isaiah 29:13-16 —

  1. How are the Pharisees like the people being described in Isaiah? (Groups)
  1. What do these verses reveal about the Pharisees’s attitude toward God?
  1. What is the law meant to do?

    It does not make us righteous, it reveals our sin.

  1. How do they shape the way the people relate to the law? Why might that cause people to stumble? (Groups)

    Keeps the people from reaching the law - Corban.

  1. What is the danger in the Pharisees’s view of themselves? (Groups)

    They are blind to their own sin. They have moved the goalposts of righteousness to make themselves feel righteous when they are the furthest thing from it.

v14-23

  1. What does Jesus say actually defiles a person?

    What comes out of them. Sin.

    1. What does it show us that out of the heart come the things that defile?

      On our own, we are bent towards sin and evil.

  1. How does Jesus feel about the law? (Groups)

    Jesus upholds the law. He rejects the traditions of the Pharisees.

Application

What is the biggest problem that the world faces?

When are we in danger of acting like the Pharisees and how can we prevent that?
