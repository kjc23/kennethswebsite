1 Peter 3:8-17

@November 2, 2023

Theme:

God blesses those who honor him in every day life and even in mistreatment.

Aim:

We are to honor and trust the Lord in how we treat those who revile us, keeping our tongues from evil, seeking to do good, and turning from evil even when it may be difficult to do so.

v8 Love God’s People

  1. Look at Philippians 2:1-11. Compare this passage to 1 Peter 3:8.
  1. Why are these 5 points in 1 Peter important?
  1. How can we practically live out these points?
  1. Which of the qualities listed in v8 do you or our church need to work on? How could you go about changing in this area?
  1. How would a Life Group look that is following the instructions in v8?

v9-12 Obtain a blessing

  1. What does it mean to bless someone?
    • To bless someone is to ask God to bestow His favor and grace upon them
  1. Highlight the instructions given to us in these verses.
  1. Why should we do each of these things?
    • 1 → To obtain a blessing
    • 2 → To see good days
    • 3 → The eyes of the Lord are on the righteous, and his ears are open to their prayer (v12)
    • A blessing is a sign that what you have done is pleasing to God and ultimately glorifies Him (helper question: Why does God bless us?)

Personal Application Questions:

  1. How difficult do we find it to bless those that mistreat us? And why?
  1. Can you think of any situations in the Bible where this happens?
  1. Can you think of ways in which you could respond to evil or insults with blessing?

v13-15 Fear God not man

  1. How would you answer the question posed in verse 13?
    • Even though there are people that can harm us physically/bodily, they can ultimately do nothing to us because we are secure in Christ and His promises.
    • At the end of the day, the suffering we endure is nothing compared to the promise of salvation and everlasting life that Jesus gives us.
  1. Verse 14b quotes from Isaiah 8v12-13. Read these verses. How does the situation that Judah is facing match that of Peter’s readers. How then should Peter’s readers respond?
    • The forces of Israel and Syria are threatening Judah and are about to attack.
    • Judah is not to fear them because God has given a prophecy to Isaiah saying that the attack will not succeed and it is Israel and Syria that will fall, so they are to fear only God.
  1. Why should we not fear those who can cause us to suffer?

v16 Defend your faith

  1. What should we be ready to do?
    • We should be ready to make a defense of our faith and the hope it provides.
    • Not everybody has to be a theologian or a scholar about every question that could be posed, but it is mainly about giving a personal reason as to why we hold this hope. How successful can our evangelism be if we do without being able to give a reason for why we believe what we do?
  1. What might this look like?
  1. How should we answer and why?
    • “do it with gentleness and respect, having a good conscience” (v15-16)
    • “so that, when you are slandered, those who revile your good behavior in Christ may be put to shame” (v16)
    • (chapter 2 v 12, 15)

V17-18 Jesus’s example

  1. Why is it better to do suffer for doing good (verse 17)?
    • God blesses those who are righteous.
    • Our ultimate priority is to do what God commands, and as we do, God satisfies our souls and bestows blessings upon us.
  1. How does Jesus exemplify what is said in verse 17?
    • Jesus was righteous, yet he suffered greatly for the unrighteous.
    • He committed no sin, yet was called a sinner and put to death.
    • It was God’s will that this would happen so that we may be saved.
  1. How does verse 18 encourage us to do the hard work of this section?
    • Jesus is the perfect example of what it looks like to be blameless yet suffer through many trials so that God’s will may be done and blessing may be given.
    • We should strive to please God in the way that Jesus has.

Closing

  1. How good are we at embodying the qualities in verse 8?
  1. How hard do we find it to avoid evil as described in verses 9-13? Is there any evil in particular that we have a hard time with?
  1. Do we feel prepared to defend our faith? Is there any area where we may need improvement or encouragement?

Summarize the passage in one sentence.
