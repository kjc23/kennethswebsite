1 Peter 2:11-25

@October 12, 2023

Passage:

Theme:

As Christians, we are to live honest and pure lives, abstaining from sin in the face of adversity and suffering just as Jesus did.

Aim:

Live honorable lives before the Lord and the world by abstaining from the passions and desires of the flesh even in the face of adversity and suffering.

Ask group what we have seen so far from the beginning of chapter 1 to 2v10.

  • Chapter 1:
    • We have an imperishable, undefiled, and unfading inheritance kept in Heaven by God
    • Angels long to look into the promises/fulfillment of the Gospel
    • We are called to be holy
    • We have been ransomed from our futile ways
    • We are to love one another
    • We are to fear God
  • Chapter 2:
    • We are to long for spiritual nourishment from God
    • Jesus is the cornerstone rejected by men but chosen and precious in the sight of God
    • Jesus is the ultimate divider (either you believe or you don’t)
    • We are like living stones being built up into a spiritual house

Have group highlight instructions given dealing with Gentiles vs. dealings with believers and God.

World:

  • Live such good lives among the pagans that though they accuse you of doing wrong… (v12)
  • Submit to every human authority (v13-14)
  • Honor the emperor (v17)
  • Slaves submit to masters (v18)

God:

  • Live as God’s slaves (v16)
  • Fear God (v17)
  • Follow in Christ’s steps (v21)

Believers:

  • Love the family of believers (v17)

v11-12 How to live Godly lives

  1. What is the situation of the people that Peter is writing to? How are the people Peter is writing to being persecuted?
    • Soft persecution of exclusion in society
    • If they don’t participate in pagan rituals they are looked down upon and judged.
    • At this point there is no state sponsored persecution
    • “When they speak against you as evil doers” (v12)
    • “Put to silence ignorance of foolish people” (v15) misinformed as well as malicious
  1. What’s the significance of using language like “waging war against your soul?” (v11)
    • Peter sees that sin is incredibly dangerous.
    • Even though we often don’t feel it, sin “wages war” against our souls
    • Peter uses this language to show us just how serious and important it is to abstain from and fight against sin.
  1. Why are we to keep our conduct honorable among the Gentiles? (v12)

    Answer is in the text explicitly, but can ask group to elaborate and converse about how keeping our conduct honorable will bring the Gentiles to glorify God.

  1. Does “waging war against your soul” line up with our view of temptation? Are there ways in which we fail to keep our conduct honorable?

    Break into small groups and let them discuss and then come back and share.

v13-17 Be subject to the State

  1. Given that in our society today, we are not subject to emperors or governors in the same way that Peter’s audience was, what kind of governing authorities are we to be subject to?
    • Presidents, MPs, King/Queen, etc.
  1. Why are we to submit to these governing powers?
    • We are told to submit to these governing powers to put to silence the ignorance of foolish people (v15)
    • “for the Lord’s sake” (v13)
    • To bring God honor
  1. How does our submission as exiles to these governing powers “put to silence the ignorance of foolish people?” (v15)
    • The Gentiles were ignorant of God, His righteousness, His law, His gospel, and His ordinances. Because of this ignorance God’s people were often slandered and censures would be put on them.
    • Peter is telling them to behave themselves in civil life so that these people may have nothing to say against them.
  1. Are there situations where it might be right to disobey the government? If so, how should it be done?
    • Yes, there may be times when the government advocates for something sinful and tries to force us into it.
    • Because our ultimate authority is God, and God commands us not to sin, we are called to disobey laws that cause us to sin.
    • Should not be done in an arrogant way, but should be done carefully.
    • Should also be done, unflinchingly. Our conviction to follow God’s commands should be so strong that we do not consider compromising on it.
  1. What do freedom and evil mean in this section? (What are we freed from? Why?)
    • Not only are we free from sin and death, God is ultimately the one we answer to. He is our ultimate authority, not men/governments. However, God calls us to submit to these authorities, not because we are enslaved to them, but because we choose to submit in order to honor God.
    • Evil means disobedience/sin. Just because we are free and don’t ultimately answer to governing authorities, but to God, it does not mean that we can pick and choose the laws we want to follow (given that they are not sinful) on the premise that we are free.
  1. How can we encourage Christians around the world who, out of reverence for God, often have to disobey their governing authorities?
    • Prayer
    • Other things
  1. How should we lovingly disagree with other Christians who have a different view of politics, government, and authority than us? What should we have in common according to these verses?
    • We should know that ultimately, we are brothers and sisters in Christ and a difference of opinion should not change the way we treat each other. We are still called to love one another from a pure heart.

v18-20 Be subject to your Masters

  1. What are our initial thoughts on this section?
  1. What do we think of Peter’s instructions to slaves regarding masters and suffering?
  1. How would this section be counter cultural in Peter’s time?
    • Peter is directly addressing slaves which nobody ever did.
  1. How are verses 18-20 counter cultural to us? Specifically in how we are to respond to those who treat us unfairly.
    • The world today says that if we are treated poorly, we should run away from whatever/whoever treated us poorly. The world encourages us to seek out justice for ourselves.
    • This section tells us to remain steadfast in trusting the Lord through suffering that is undeserved. We are not to retaliate, because ultimately God defines who we are and we know that Jesus suffered far more for us.

v21-25 The Transforming Power of the Cross

  1. *Interactive question: In what ways does Jesus exemplify everything that we’ve been talking about thus far in the passage?

    Have someone write out what people shout out.

  1. What is the end result of following this example?
    • Salvation
    • and glorifying God

Conclusion/Application

  1. How can the cross of Christ become more central to our witness in the workplace through our lifestyle and through our lips?

    Let people discuss for a time.

  1. Summarize this passage in 1-2 sentences.

    Split into groups for a couple of minutes and then compare.
