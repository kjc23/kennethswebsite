Mark 9:30-10:12

@February 20, 2025

Theme:

Discipleship means repenting of sin and becoming a servant of all – these men need a heart change!

Aim:

Disciples are to become the servants of all, rejecting sin following Christ in all things.

Introduction

What are your best motivators? (In work, relationships, spiritual life, etc.) (What motivates you to do well?)

What has Jesus said will happen to Him in 8:31?

What does it mean to be a disciple (8:34-38)?

— Read Passage. Highlight anything that stands out, questions, etc. —

v30-32

  1. Compare 8:31-32 with 9:30-32. What is similar and what is different? (Groups)

v33-40

  1. What do the disciples think the Kingdom of God is about? Greatness and power
  1. What does Jesus say the Kingdom is actually about? Humility and service
    • Helper question: How does a child compare to one of Jesus’s disciples (in terms of status)?
  1. What are the disciples still getting wrong? They are still trying to do things by their own power
  1. What is Mark showing us about true discipleship? (Groups)

v41-50

  1. What phrase is repeated 4 times is the focus of Jesus words here? Cause to sin/stumble
  1. Why is causing someone to stumble such a serious thing?
  1. What would happen if we tried to live out 42-47? (Groups)
  1. So what conclusion are we meant to take away instead?
  1. Why is the disciples attitude in the previous section so dangerous?
  1. What is the important thing for disciples to take away from Jesus words about hell?

10v1-12

  1. What does the pharisees attitude to the law go wrong?
  1. What is the common problem between pharisees and disciples

Application

Why do our motivations matter so much?
Where is this passage most challenging my motives? (in work, evangelism, bible reading etc.)
Why is it important to understand the severity of our sin?
How is Jesus calling me to grow as a disciple through this passage?
