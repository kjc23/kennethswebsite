1 Peter 4:12-19

@November 16, 2023

Theme:

Those who suffer as Christians should not be ashamed but should glorify God knowing that His Spirit is upon them and will bring them safely through His judgement.

Aim:

When we suffer, it should be as Christians, rather than because of our sin knowing that to suffer for the name of Christ is a blessing because He has come to save us.

General Questions

  1. Thus far in 1 Peter, what have we learned about the sufferings of Jesus?

    Split off into groups and write down on a piece of paper what you find

    • Predicted by the Prophets (1v11)
    • Brings salvation (1v9-10)
    • Ultimately brings glory “subsequent glories” (1v11)
    • It is an example (2v21)
    • He committed no sin (2v22)
    • He did not return reviling He received (2v23)
    • He entrusted himself to him who judges justly (2v23)
    • He bore our sins (2v24)
    • We have been healed by His suffering (2v24)
    • One time suffering (3v18)
  1. What have we seen about the sufferings of Christians so far?
    • Trials that test genuineness of faith (1v6-7)
    • Pressure to indulge in passions of flesh (2v11)
    • Accused of being evil (2v12)
    • Gracious thing in the sight of God to suffer for doing good (2v20)
    • You will be blessed when you suffer for righteousness sake (3v14)
    • Should not be a surprise (4v12)
    • We share in Christs sufferings (4v13)
    • We are blessed because the Spirit of Glory rests upon us (4v14)
    • Do not be ashamed of suffering for Christ (4v15)
    • Do so because judgment is coming
  1. Looking at 4v12-19, add any new points to the previous lists. (highlighted in blue)

v12-13

  1. Why should we not be surprised when trials come our way?
    • God has not promised that as Christians our lives will be perfectly easy, in fact quite the opposite as we’ve seen from the whole of 1 Peter.
    • However, God has promised us His salvation and has made himself known to us. He is always present with us even through fiery trials.
  1. Do we tend to be surprised when trials come our way? Why?

    Application question, let people discuss for a bit.

  1. Why does Peter tell us instead to rejoice when we are faced with trials?
    • “insofar as you share in Christ’s sufferings, that you may also be glad when his glory is revealed” (v13)
    • We will be filled with joy and gladness at seeing the Lord’s glory revealed.
  1. What do we tend to do when faced with trials? Are we surprised? Or are we still able to rejoice?

    Application question, discuss.

v14-16

  1. What does what we suffer for show us?
    • The things we suffer for can be revealing of our priorities/character.
    • If we suffer “as a murderer or a thief or an evildoer or as a meddler” it is a marker of our sinful desires.
    • If we suffer for Christ, it is a marker of His Spirit upon us.
  1. Why does Peter use the language of “Spirit of Glory” (some translations: “Spirit of Power”)?
    • When we go through trials/suffer and we are able to rejoice through it by the power of the Spirit, it brings glory to God.
    • Similarly, it is God’s spirit that gives us the strength and power to endure suffering in a way that is glorifying to God.

v17-19

  1. What kind of judgement is Peter talking about in these verses?
    • This judgement seems to be twofold
    • Peter is both talking about a final judgement at the end of times (v7: “the end of all things are at hand”) as well as the daily judgement/sufferings that Christians face (1v6-7).
    • I think he is mainly speaking about fiery trials that come to test/refine our faith
  1. In the context of daily sufferings, why does judgement begin at the House of God?
    • God allows us to go through trials in order to refine us and strengthen our faith (1v6-7)
    • In this, we are prepared for God’s final judgement
  1. Why does Peter use the words “faithful Creator?” And have we seen language similar to this previously in 1 Peter?
    • Shepherd and Overseer of your souls (2v25)
    • Peter reminds his readers that God is a personal Creator
    • And He is and will always be faithful.

Application Questions

  1. Are we ashamed when we suffer as Christians?
  1. How can we go about rejoicing more especially when faced with trials?
  1. How does this passage help us to pray for the global suffering church (especially those that are truly suffering for being Christians)?

Ending

Summarize the passage in one sentence.
